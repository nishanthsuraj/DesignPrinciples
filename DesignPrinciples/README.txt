When it comes to software design principles, there are several guidelines that help software engineers create robust, maintainable, and
efficient systems. Let’s explore some of these principles:
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Should not suffer from "Tunnel Vision”:
While designing, avoid focusing solely on completing the immediate goal. Consider broader effects and implications.
Ensure that the design aligns with the overall system requirements and goals.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Traceable to Analysis Model:
The design process should be directly linked to the initial analysis model.
It should satisfy all the requirements necessary for developing a high-quality software product.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Avoid "Reinventing the Wheel":
Don’t waste time or effort creating components or solutions that already exist.
Reuse existing patterns, libraries, and best practices.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Minimize Intellectual Distance:
Bridge the gap between real-world problems and software solutions.
Make the design intuitive and straightforward for developers and users.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Exhibit Uniformity and Integration:
Maintain consistency throughout the design.
Integrate subsystems seamlessly into a cohesive whole.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Accommodate Change:
Design software that can adapt to evolving requirements.
Flexibility is crucial to accommodate future enhancements.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Degrade Gracefully:
Even in the face of errors or unexpected situations, the software should continue functioning.
Avoid abrupt failures; gracefully handle exceptions.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Assess for Quality:
Evaluate the design rigorously.
Focus on aspects like maintainability, scalability, and performance.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Review to Discover Errors:
Regularly review the design to identify any flaws or areas for improvement.
Catch issues early in the process.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. Remember: Design Is Not Coding, and Coding Is Not Design:
Design involves planning, structuring, and describing the logic of the program.
Coding is the implementation of the design using a specific programming language 1.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These principles guide software engineers in creating effective and reliable software systems. Keep them in mind during your design process! 🚀
